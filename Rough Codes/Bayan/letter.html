Contractor/Contractor Agreement (Salaried)

This Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between Bayaan Academy Inc. (hereinafter referred to as "Academy"), a private school registered in the United States of America, and Test Patel Patel, an independent contractor (hereinafter referred to as “Contractor”, "Contractor" or “you”). The Academy and Contractor agree to the following terms and conditions:

  1. Term of Agreement: The term of this Agreement is from August 1, 2024 to July 31, 2025 . Contracts terminate at the end of their term and must be mutually renewed.
  2. Relationship of the Parties: You are an independent contractor of the Academy, and this Agreement shall not be construed to create any employment or agency relationship between you and the Academy. You shall not make any agreements or representations on the Academy’s behalf without the Academy’s prior written consent nor do you have the authority to bind the Academy.
  3. Contractor Responsibilities: You agree to conduct work towards the betterment of the Academy in accordance with all applicable laws, policies, and guidelines and to cooperate with the Academy. You will strive to embody God-consciousness (taqwa) and follow Islamic ideals while conducting any activity on behalf of the Academy, whether online or at onsite events/classes.
  4. Schedule and Availability: You agree to be available for work Monday-Thursday: 9am-5:15pm (EST), and Sun: 9am-12pm (EST). Any changes to the work schedule or addition of scope of work must be mutually agreed up on in writing.
  5. Compensation: The agreed-upon compensation for you is $0.00 USD per month . Details are as follows:
  6. Paid holidays marked on the academic calendar (such Eid breaks and end of Ramadan break).
  7. Wages are sent monthly for hours worked and is processed on the last Fri of the month.
  8. Scope of work: Contractor is responsible for using their knowledge and skill to create engaging lessons and projects, attending Sunday meeting for updates or collaboration, collaboration meetings throughout the week, grading assignments, providing feedback, communicating with students or parents as needed, and any other related duties.
  9. Payment Processing: Contractor shall be responsible for creating an account with the Academy’s payroll service or another mutually agreeable entity for the transfer of monthly payments. Payments will be processed on the last Friday of each month.
  10. Tax Liability: Contractor shall pay all taxes incurred while performing services under this Agreement, including all applicable income taxes and self-employment taxes. You shall indemnify the Academy against all such taxes or contributions, including penalties and interest. Upon demand, you shall provide the Academy with proof that such payments have been made. Contractor must fill and return IRS Tax Form W-9, which will be provided to you. The Academy will provide Contractor with a IRS Form 1099-NEC prior January 31, 2025 and 2026. Contractor further acknowledges that the Academy will not:
  11. Withhold Federal Insurance Contributions Act (“FICA”) (Social Security and Medicare taxes) from Contractor’s payments, or make FICA payments on Contractor's behalf;
  12. Make state or federal unemployment compensation contributions on Contractor’s behalf; or
  13. Withhold state or federal income tax from Contractor’s payments.
  14. All International Contractors are responsible for their own governmental taxes.
  15. Fringe Benefits: Contractor understands that Contractor is not eligible for health insurance, vacation pay, sick leave, or any other fringe benefits. Contractor further understands that the Academy shall not obtain workers’ compensation insurance on behalf of Contractor.
  16. Confidentiality: Contractor acknowledges that during the course of this Agreement, they will have access to confidential information that is important for the functioning of the Academy, both financially and operationally. All documents, contacts, and processes are confidential and shall not be shared with anyone outside the Academy, except with administrative staff for the benefit of the Academy. These confidential items include, but are not limited to, information about students (discipline issues, contact info, etc.), parent and teacher contact information (email, mail, phone, etc.), marketing materials, handbooks, curriculum, course materials, accreditation documentation, internal discussions about the Academy (and its teachers, students, parents, and staff), Academy’s platform services and logins. Contractor acknowledges these confidential items are worthy of protection and are the sole property of the Academy. Violation of such confidentiality could lead to the Academy seeking litigation for damages.
  17. Non-Solicitation: Contractor agrees that during the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) consecutive months following the termination or separation from the Academy, for whatever reason, they shall not directly or indirectly employ, attempt to employ, or solicit for employment any other staff of the Academy for any business or entity providing the same or similar services as the Academy.
  18. Notice of Termination of Contract: Either party may terminate the Agreement at any time; however, we request at least a 3-weeks notice to be given thus allowing the Academy reasonable time to find a suitable resolution or replacement.
  19. Conflicts of Interest: Bayaan Academy Inc. is concerned with conflicts of interest that create actual or potential work-related concerns, especially in the areas of confidentiality, customer relations, safety, security, and morale. If you, as an independent Contractor, have any actual or potential conflict of interest with a competitor, supplier, distributor, or Contractor of the Academy, you must disclose it to the Administrators. If an actual or potential conflict of interest is determined to exist, the Academy will take the necessary steps to reduce or eliminate this conflict.
  20. Ethics Code: The confidentiality of trade secrets, proprietary information, and similar confidential commercially-sensitive information (i.e. financial or sales records/reports, marketing or business strategies/plans, product development, customer lists, patents, trademarks, etc.) about the Academy or operations, or that of our customers or partners, is to be treated with discretion and only disseminated on a need-to-know basis (see policies relating to privacy). Violation of the Code of Ethics can result in termination of this contract and possible litigation if harm to Academy.
  21. Identity Verification: All Contractors are required to complete and submit all relevant documentation that prove their identity and place of residence. Contractors are also required to submit a criminal background check from their home country or country of residence prior to starting.
  22. Relatives and Friends: It is the obligation of the Contractor to disclose their relationship to any other Contractor that is currently contracted or is being contracted by Bayaan. Bayaan will evaluate if an actual or potential conflict may arise that could compromise supervision, safety, confidentiality, security, and moral. Bayaan Academy will determine the appropriate course of action.
  23. Attendance: Bayaan Academy Inc requires Contractors to maintain their agreed upon working times by logging in at the scheduled times for instruction, deliver high quality lessons diligently and professionally. In the event they will not be able to attend, they must inform the relevant administrator so a substitute can be found to cover the instruction. If you are not able to carry your instructional duties due to an emergency or other unexpected circumstance, notify your Administrators as soon as possible. Notice should include the expected duration of your absence and your expected date of return. If you become ill and are not able to teach, notify your Administrators immediately. If you fail to provide instruction for 3 days or more consecutive days and have not provided proper notification, the Academy will assume that you have voluntarily terminated your contract.
  24. Recording Time: Contractors must follow established Academy procedures for recording hours worked and report them accurately and fairly on a regular basis. Deliberately falsifying hours worked can lead to the termination of the contract.
  25. Governing Law, Jurisdiction, and Venue: This Agreement and all matters arising out of or relating to this Agreement and the Services provided hereunder, for all purposes, shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the law of the State of Maryland, without giving effect to any conflict of laws principles that would cause the laws of any other jurisdiction to apply.
  26. Complete Agreement: This Agreement supersedes all prior contracts and agreements between Contractor and Academy and may not be modified, changed, or altered by either the Academy or the Contractor. Any modification, change, or alteration of this Agreement must be in writing and signed by both the Contractor and the Academy Director, Principal, or Board member. Should any part of this Agreement be found unenforceable by law, the remainder of the contract shall remain in full force.
